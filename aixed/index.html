The problem

AI moves fast. The signal is lost in the noise.

AI is reshaping classrooms, campuses, curricula, and policy. But the faster it grows, the harder it becomes to follow. What's out there is overwhelming, fragmented, and inconsistent — full of hype, uncertainty, and noise — and the people who have to make real decisions about it don't have time to sift.

Worldwide search interest in “AI news”, 2010–2025. Source: Google Trends

The gap

There's no trusted home for AI in education.

Education leaders, teachers and faculty, edtech teams, and researchers all struggle to tell what really matters. They spend hours sifting through headlines and opinion pieces — or give up because it's just too much. What's missing is a home for content that's insightful but accessible, credible but not academic, strategic but still practical.

Why it matters

It's AI-first now — yet almost no one feels ready.

Infrastructure investment is booming, adoption is climbing, and AI hiring has surged. The message is clear — and so is the gap between intent and readiness.
